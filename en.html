Features

We only removed the waiting and the sign-up form — the analysis is all here

RideLens reads the raw binary FIT files your Garmin or Wahoo records. No conversion, no upload queue.

Analysis done in 3 seconds

Drop a FIT/GPX file and it's parsed instantly. Tens of thousands of track points, processed right in your browser.

NP · IF · TSS

Enter your FTP and get Normalized Power, Intensity Factor and Training Stress Score computed on the spot.

Synced interactive charts

Move your cursor across the elevation, speed, power and heart-rate charts — all four charts and the map point to the same moment.

Route map & gallery

Your GPS track drawn as recorded, and your library organized as a gallery of route thumbnails. Exporting FIT to GPX is one click.

Share cards for social

A share image with your stats, route and elevation is rendered on your device — send it straight to Instagram or your group chat.

Automatic climb analysis

Climbs are detected automatically with length, gradient and VAM — and graded HC to Cat 4, Tour de France style.

Rider card & growth system

Are you a climber or a sprinter? Your abilities are rated, and levels, XP and achievements stack up like you're raising a game character.

Riding heatmap

A one-year grid where every riding day turns green, with a streak counter for consecutive weeks. The empty squares will start calling you.

Virtual cross-country journey

Your cumulative distance travels Korea's 633 km Incheon→Busan trail. "Passing Ihwaryeong now, 218 km to Busan" — finish it and start lap two.

Year-in-review card

Your annual distance as "Seoul→Busan × N" and climbing as "Everest × N" — a whole year of riding on one card, made to be shown off.

Exploration log · gear · stamps

Regions you ride get recorded in your atlas, passing a bike-path certification center earns a stamp, and weekly quests unlock limited gear.

Bike garage — part replacement alerts

Every ride records which bike you rode, and mileage accumulates automatically. Pro manages multiple bikes and warns when your chain, tires or tubes are due — no more "when did I last change the chain?"

My goals — rewards for hitting them

Set "3 rides a week" or "300 km a month" and every saved ride counts toward it automatically. Hit the goal for XP rewards, keep it going for streaks — one more reason to ride consistently.

Ride planner — "can I make it?"

Enter distance and elevation (or a GPX course) and get an answer based on your own pace, learned from your rides — a 4-level finish-likelihood verdict, ETA, how often to rest, how much water and food to carry, and whether you'll be back before sunset. Or pick from curated regional routes and the community's most popular courses.

Rest stop map — shared by riders

Toilets, water fountains, cafés, convenience stores and viewpoints good for a cycling break — the places Naver and Google Maps don't gather. Browse near your route, add your own, upvote the good ones. Han river toilets and water points come pre-loaded.

Virtual battles with friends

Trade links and battle across five ability stats, then share the result as a VS card. You can also race your past self — or your all-time PRs.

Outlier detection

If a car-transported track sneaks in, you get an instant "6× your usual" warning — so bad data never pollutes your personal records.

Analysis without the leak worry

GPS tracks reveal where you live; heart rate reveals your health. RideLens never sends that file anywhere, so there's nowhere for it to leak from — only when you create a share link is a summary kept for 30 days, then auto-deleted.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Does my file really go nowhere?

Yes. RideLens is a static web page and all analysis code runs inside your browser. Open your dev tools' Network tab while analyzing a file — you won't see a single file upload request.

Full honest disclosure: when you open the app, at most once a day, an anonymous visit-count signal is sent to our server, and pressing a major button sends just that button's name (at most once a day). No cookies, no identifiers, no personal data, no ride data — only flags like "first visit today" — and no third-party analytics like Google Analytics. Details are in the app under Help → Service Notice.

Is the app available in English?

Yes — the main interface (home, report, library controls) is available in English (beta). If your browser isn't set to Korean it opens in English automatically, and you can switch anytime with the EN / 한국어 button in the header. Deeper content (help articles, quests, region names) is still being translated.

Which devices are supported?

Any device that records standard FIT files — Garmin Edge, Forerunner, Fenix, Wahoo ELEMNT and more. GPX files exported from Strava, Komoot and similar services also work.

How do I export a file from Strava?

On the activity page, open the ⋯ menu → "Export GPX". If you need the original FIT, choose "Export Original". You can also plug your device into a PC and grab files straight from the GARMIN/Activity folder.

How far does the free tier go?

All analysis is free — NP/IF/TSS, climb grading, the rider card (abilities and levels), achievements, the riding heatmap, the virtual cross-country journey, year-in-review cards, exploration and stamp collecting, one bike (per-ride tagging and mileage), one goal, the ride planner (finish likelihood, rest and supplies), your library and share cards. No time limit.

Pro unlocks "deeper" — multiple bikes with part replacement alerts and per-bike stats, three goals, growth quests, ride-over-ride comparisons, PDF reports, training load, and cross-device sync.

Can I install it as an app?

Yes. Add it to your home screen (Android and desktop get an install prompt; on iPhone use Safari's Share → Add to Home Screen) and it opens from an icon with no address bar. On desktop the installed app also becomes a file handler — double-click a .fit/.gpx and it opens straight into a report. Installing changes nothing else: your data still lives only on your device.

What happens to my records when I switch devices?

One tap on "📲 Move to another device" in your library creates a move link — open it on the new device and every ride, level, badge, bike, goal and your Pro license comes across intact. The link works once, within 48 hours, and the server only briefly holds ciphertext locked by a code that never leaves your link. Free for everyone.

How do I get my key after buying Pro?

There's nothing to wait for — when checkout finishes you're returned to the RideLens app automatically, and Pro turns on instantly with no key entry. Your license key is also shown on screen; jot it down if you plan to use other devices (3 devices total).

What if I buy Pro and don't like it?

Within 14 days of purchase you get a full refund, no questions asked. One email is all it takes.

How do I cancel? Do I lose my records?

One click on the management link in your receipt email cancels the subscription. Even after cancelling, the free features and everything in your library — rides, levels, badges — remain untouched, because the data was always on your device to begin with.
